--- layout: main title: Working with periods --- Periods ======= The main feature of CalendR is the time period manipulation. There is 5 Period types available for the moment : * Year * Month * Week * Day * Range All theese class are under the `CalendR\Period` namespace ### Retrieve a period Excepting the range class, all the period types can be generated using the CalendR factory, wich is the simplest way. {% highlight php %} getYear($year); $month = $factory->getYear($year, $month); $week = $factory->getYear($year, $weeknum); $day = $factory->getYear($year, $month, $day); ?> {% endhighlight %} ### Iterating over a period You can iterate other Year, Month, and Week. Iterating over Year gives you Months Iterating over Month gives you Weeks, but you can use the getDays() method to iterate over days Iterating over Week gives you Days This allows you to create a calendars in seconds : {% highlight php %} getMonth(2012, 01); ?>
